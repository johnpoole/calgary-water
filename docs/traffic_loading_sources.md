# Traffic loading → buried water pipe risk (sources)

Saved: 2025-12-31

## Key idea
Traffic loading can introduce external stress/strain on buried water mains and is commonly cited as a contributor to deterioration and failures, especially under heavy/arterial traffic.

## Sources
- **Rajani & Zhan (2022)** — *Engineering Failure Analysis*  
  Buried water pipes experience stress from traffic loading, which contributes to deterioration and failure.  
  https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0886779822001031

- **Yang et al. (2024)** — *Mechanical performance of buried pipe under traffic load* (PMC)  
  Traffic loads induce measurable stress and strain in buried pipes.  
  https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12551904/

- **Mielczarek & Klos (2018)** — *The Unfavourable Impact of Street Traffic on Water Distribution Networks* (MDPI Water)  
  Urban case study: higher failure occurrence on streets with intense traffic.  
  https://www.mdpi.com/2073-4441/10/8/1086

- **Kleiner & Rajani (2001+)** — NRC / review papers  
  External loads (including traffic) are listed as key deterioration drivers in failure risk models.  
  https://nrc-publications.canada.ca/eng/view/accepted/?id=b2c5934a-3fec-4c31-95ff-cd85ee04dd77

- **Zhu et al. (2022)** — *Truck Impact on Buried Water Pipes* (NSF PAR)  
  Traffic loading modeled as a significant factor in buried pipe risk.  
  https://par.nsf.gov/servlets/purl/10354626
